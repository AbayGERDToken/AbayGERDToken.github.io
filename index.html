    Fun Fact:

    Laszlo Hanyecz was a computer programmer from Florida who made history for making the first documented purchase of a physical item using Bitcoin. On May 22, 2010, Hanyecz offered to pay 10,000 Bitcoins for two pizzas on the BitcoinTalk forum. The value of 10,000 Bitcoins at that time was roughly $41. A forum user in the UK agreed to take up the offer and ordered two pizzas from Papa John's for Hanyecz in exchange for the 10,000 Bitcoins. These two pizzas have gone down in history as likely being the most expensive pizzas ever bought.

    The day is now commemorated annually as "Bitcoin Pizza Day". It's an event that's held in high regard within the cryptocurrency community, as it was a milestone that helped to legitimize Bitcoin as a means of payment. At the same time, it's also a stark reminder of Bitcoin's incredible price appreciation over a relatively short period of time.

    As of May 2023, the value of those 10,000 Bitcoins would be well over $250 million dollars, assuming Bitcoin's price is roughly around $25,000 per coin.
